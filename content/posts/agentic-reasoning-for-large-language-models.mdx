## Motivation

大型语言模型（LLMs）在封闭世界中展现出强大的推理能力，但在开放式和动态环境中却面临挑战。传统的LLM推理方法通常是静态的，难以适应不断变化的环境和长期交互。

为了解决这一局限性，研究人员开始将LLM视为自主智能体，使其能够通过规划、行动和学习来适应复杂环境。这种“智能体推理”范式旨在弥合LLM的强大语言理解与现实世界动态交互之间的鸿沟。

本文旨在系统地组织和回顾智能体推理领域，为研究人员提供一个统一的框架和未来研究方向的路线图。

## Motivation

Large Language Models (LLMs) demonstrate strong reasoning capabilities in controlled, closed-world settings. However, they struggle significantly when faced with open-ended and dynamic environments, where traditional static reasoning approaches fall short.

To address these limitations, researchers are shifting paradigms, reframing LLMs as autonomous agents. These agents are designed to plan, act, and learn through continuous interaction, thereby bridging the gap between powerful language understanding and real-world dynamic engagement.

This survey aims to systematically organize and review the emerging field of agentic reasoning, providing a unified framework and a roadmap for future research directions.

## Contribution

- **Unified Agentic Reasoning Framework**: Presents a framework organizing agentic reasoning along three complementary dimensions: environmental dynamics (foundational, self-evolving, multi-agent), reasoning modes (in-context vs. post-training), and application domains.
- **Layered Environmental Dynamics**: Characterizes environmental dynamics through three layers: foundational agentic reasoning (core single-agent capabilities), self-evolving agentic reasoning (refining capabilities through feedback, memory, and adaptation), and collective multi-agent reasoning (coordination, knowledge sharing).
- **Distinction of Reasoning Modes**: Clearly distinguishes between in-context reasoning, which scales test-time interaction through structured orchestration, and post-training reasoning, which optimizes behaviors via reinforcement learning and supervised fine-tuning.
- **Comprehensive Review of Applications and Benchmarks**: Reviews representative agentic reasoning frameworks across real-world applications and benchmarks, including science, robotics, healthcare, autonomous research, and mathematics.
- **Roadmap for Future Research**: Outlines open challenges and future directions for agentic reasoning, including personalization, long-horizon interaction, world modeling, scalable multi-agent training, and governance for real-world deployment.

## Method

As a survey paper, this work primarily organizes and presents the field of agentic reasoning through the following structure:

### 1. Defining the Paradigm Shift
- **Agentic Reasoning Definition**: Reconceptualizes LLMs as autonomous agents capable of planning, acting, and learning to navigate open-ended and dynamic environments.
- **Contrast with Traditional LLM Reasoning**: Highlights the advantages of agentic reasoning in adaptability and interactivity compared to static LLM approaches.

### 2. Three-Dimensional Organizing Framework
- **Environmental Dynamics**:
    1. **Foundational Agentic Reasoning**: Focuses on core single-agent capabilities like planning, tool use, and search in stable environments.
    2. **Self-Evolving Agentic Reasoning**: Explores how agents refine capabilities through feedback, memory, and adaptation.
    3. **Collective Multi-Agent Reasoning**: Extends intelligence to collaborative settings involving coordination, knowledge sharing, and shared goals.
- **Reasoning Modes**:
    - **In-context Reasoning**: Scales test-time interaction through structured orchestration.
    - **Post-training Reasoning**: Optimizes agent behaviors via RL and SFT.
- **Application Domains**: Covers areas such as science, robotics, healthcare, autonomous research, and mathematics.

### 3. Review of Representative Frameworks and Applications
- **Planning Systems**: Examines agentic planning systems categorized by modality, structure, format, and tool.
- **Tool-Use Optimization**: Analyzes systems based on their integration stage, learning type, and tool strategy.
- **Other Key Components**: Discusses aspects like memory, world modeling, and multi-agent collaboration.

### 4. Open Challenges and Future Directions
- **Personalization**: How agents can adapt to individual users or specific tasks.
- **Long-Horizon Interaction**: Addressing long-term dependencies in complex, multi-step tasks.
- **World Modeling**: How agents build and update their understanding of the environment.
- **Scalable Multi-Agent Training**: Efficiently training and coordinating large numbers of agents.
- **Governance**: Ensuring safety, fairness, and transparency in real-world agent deployment.

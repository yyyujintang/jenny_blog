## Motivation

在专业环境中，长周期、重复性的工作流程非常普遍，例如处理费用报销或录入学生成绩。这些任务对人类来说往往枯燥乏味，且耗时漫长。

然而，这类任务对于计算机使用代理（CUA）来说却非常理想。它们通常包含结构化、重复的子工作流程，其逻辑可以被系统地学习。

当前缺乏一个专门的评估基准来衡量CUA在这些复杂、长周期任务中的表现，这成为了该领域发展的主要瓶颈。

## Contribution

*   **OS-Marathon基准的建立**: 提出了一个包含242个长周期、重复性任务的基准，涵盖两个领域，用于评估SOTA代理。
*   **成本效益高的演示构建方法**: 引入了一种使用少量示例构建精简演示的方法，以教授代理底层工作流程逻辑。
*   **有效执行复杂工作流程**: 使代理能够有效地在更大、未见过的数据集上执行类似的工作流程。
*   **揭示固有挑战与方法有效性**: 通过广泛的实验，展示了这些任务固有的挑战以及所提出方法的有效性。

## Method

### OS-Marathon基准设计
1.  **任务选择**: 收集了专业环境中常见的长周期、重复性任务，如费用报销和成绩录入。
2.  **领域覆盖**: 基准涵盖了两个主要领域，以确保任务的多样性和通用性。
3.  **任务数量**: 包含242个任务，旨在提供全面的评估。
4.  **难度分级**: 任务根据数据量和每实例处理复杂性分为七个难度级别。

### 演示构建方法
1.  **少量示例**: 使用few-shot示例来演示任务的工作流程。
2.  **精简演示**: 采用成本效益高的方法来构建一个浓缩的演示，突出核心逻辑。
3.  **逻辑学习**: 代理通过这些精简的演示学习任务的底层工作流程逻辑。
4.  **泛化能力**: 学习到的逻辑使代理能够泛化到更大的、未见过的数据集上。

## Motivation

Long-horizon, repetitive workflows are common in professional settings, such as processing expense reports or entering student grades. These tasks are often tedious and time-consuming for humans.

However, such tasks are ideal for Computer-Use Agents (CUAs) due to their structured, recurring sub-workflows with logic that can be systematically learned.

A primary bottleneck in this field is the absence of a dedicated evaluation benchmark to measure CUA performance on these complex, long-horizon tasks.

## Contribution

*   **Establishment of OS-Marathon Benchmark**: Introduced a benchmark comprising 242 long-horizon, repetitive tasks across two domains to evaluate state-of-the-art (SOTA) agents.
*   **Cost-Effective Demonstration Construction**: Proposed a method to construct condensed demonstrations using only few-shot examples to teach agents underlying workflow logic.
*   **Effective Execution of Complex Workflows**: Enabled agents to effectively execute similar workflows on larger, unseen data collections.
*   **Demonstration of Inherent Challenges and Method Effectiveness**: Extensive experiments revealed both the inherent challenges of these tasks and the effectiveness of the proposed method.

## Method

### OS-Marathon Benchmark Design
1.  **Task Selection**: Collected common long-horizon, repetitive tasks from professional settings, such as expense report processing and grade entry.
2.  **Domain Coverage**: The benchmark spans two main domains to ensure task diversity and generality.
3.  **Task Volume**: Comprises 242 tasks, designed to provide comprehensive evaluation.
4.  **Difficulty Levels**: Tasks are organized into seven difficulty levels based on data volume and per-instance processing complexity.

### Demonstration Construction Method
1.  **Few-Shot Examples**: Utilizes a small number of examples to demonstrate the task workflow.
2.  **Condensed Demonstration**: Employs a cost-effective method to build a condensed demonstration that highlights the core logic.
3.  **Logic Learning**: Agents learn the underlying workflow logic of the tasks from these condensed demonstrations.
4.  **Generalization Capability**: The learned logic allows agents to generalize effectively to larger, unseen data collections.

## Comparison with Previous Work

*   **vs. Existing Benchmarks**: Current benchmarks typically focus on short-horizon, one-off tasks, lacking evaluation for long-horizon, repetitive workflows. OS-Marathon fills this gap.
*   **vs. Traditional RL Environments**: Traditional RL environments are often simulated or highly structured, whereas OS-Marathon tasks are closer to real-world computer-use scenarios, involving more complex UI interactions and data processing.
*   **vs. Focus on Single-Step Accuracy**: Many existing evaluation metrics only focus on the accuracy of individual steps, while OS-Marathon emphasizes end-to-end accuracy and efficiency across the entire long-horizon workflow.

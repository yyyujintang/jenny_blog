## Motivation

AI agents are increasingly operating over extended time horizons, yet their ability to retain, organize, and recall multimodal experiences remains a critical bottleneck.
Building effective lifelong memory requires navigating a vast design space spanning architecture, retrieval strategies, prompt engineering, and data pipelines. This space is too large and interconnected for manual exploration or traditional AutoML to explore effectively.

## Contribution

- **Autonomous Research Pipeline**: Deployed an autonomous research pipeline that, starting from a naive baseline, autonomously executes approximately 50 experiments across two benchmarks, diagnosing failure modes, proposing architectural modifications, and repairing data pipeline bugs, all without human intervention in the inner loop.
- **Omni-SimpleMem Framework**: Discovered and proposed Omni-SimpleMem, a unified multimodal memory framework for lifelong AI agents.
- **Significant Performance Improvement**: Achieved state-of-the-art on both benchmarks, improving F1 by +411% on LoCoMo (0.117$\to$0.598) and +214% on Mem-Gallery (0.254$\to$0.797).
- **Beyond Traditional AutoML**: Demonstrated that bug fixes, architectural changes, and prompt engineering contributions individually exceed the cumulative contribution of all hyperparameter tuning, showcasing capabilities fundamentally beyond the reach of traditional AutoML.
- **Taxonomy of Discovery Types and Applicability Analysis**: Provided a taxonomy of six discovery types and identified four properties that make multimodal memory particularly suited for autoresearch, offering guidance for applying autonomous research pipelines to other AI system domains.

## Method

This paper deploys an autonomous research pipeline to discover Omni-SimpleMem.
1.  **Initial Configuration**: Starting from a naive baseline (F1=0.117 on LoCoMo).
2.  **Experiment Execution**: The pipeline autonomously executes approximately 50 experiments across two benchmarks.
3.  **Failure Diagnosis**: Automatically identifies and diagnoses failure modes within the system.
4.  **Architectural Modification Proposal**: Proposes architectural modifications to improve performance.
5.  **Bug Repair**: Automatically identifies and repairs data pipeline bugs.
6.  **Iterative Optimization**: Continuously iterates and optimizes the system without human intervention in the inner loop.
7.  **Discovery Types**: Identifies six main discovery types, including bug fixes, architectural changes, prompt engineering, format alignment, data repair, and hyperparameter tuning.

## Comparison with Previous Work

- **vs. Manual Exploration**: Traditional manual exploration cannot effectively navigate the vast and interconnected design space, whereas the autonomous research pipeline systematically explores and discovers critical improvements.
- **vs. Traditional AutoML**: Traditional AutoML primarily focuses on hyperparameter tuning. This research demonstrates that bug fixes, architectural changes, and prompt engineering contribute far more than hyperparameter tuning, showcasing capabilities beyond traditional AutoML.
- **vs. Existing Memory Models (e.g., MemVerse, MemGPT, SimpleMem)**: Omni-SimpleMem significantly outperforms existing SOTA models, including SimpleMem, on both benchmarks, with F1 scores improving by +411% on LoCoMo and +214% on Mem-Gallery.

## Motivation

哺乳动物大脑能存储大量知识并持续整合新信息，同时避免灾难性遗忘，以适应不断变化的环境。

尽管大型语言模型（LLM）取得了显著成就，即使结合RAG，在预训练后仍难以高效、有效地整合大量新经验。

现有方法在处理多跳问答和需要深度知识整合的场景时，往往效率低下且性能受限。

## Contribution

- **神经生物学启发的新型检索框架**: 引入HippoRAG，模仿人脑海马体在长期记忆中的索引作用，实现LLM更深层次、更高效的知识整合。
- **LLM、知识图谱与Personalized PageRank的协同**: 创新性地结合LLM、知识图谱（KG）和Personalized PageRank (PPR) 算法，模拟大脑皮层和海马体在记忆中的不同角色。
- **显著提升多跳问答性能**: 在多跳问答任务上，HippoRAG的性能比现有SOTA方法高出20%。
- **高效率与低成本**: 单步检索的HippoRAG与迭代检索方法（如IRCoT）性能相当或更优，但成本降低10-30倍，速度提高6-13倍。
- **解决新类型场景的能力**: 能够处理现有方法无法应对的新型复杂场景。

## Method

HippoRAG通过以下步骤模仿人脑海马体的记忆机制：

1.  **构建知识图谱 (Knowledge Graph Construction)**:
    *   利用LLM（如GPT-3.5 Turbo）从原始文本语料中提取三元组（主语-谓语-宾语），构建一个初步的知识图谱。
    *   通过实体链接和指代消解，确保图谱中的实体一致性。
    *   添加同义词边，将语义相似的节点连接起来，增强图谱的连接性。
2.  **查询扩展与图谱激活 (Query Expansion and Graph Activation)**:
    *   对于给定的查询，LLM首先生成相关的实体和概念作为初始查询节点。
    *   这些查询节点在知识图谱中被激活，作为Personalized PageRank的起始点。
3.  **Personalized PageRank (PPR) 检索**:
    *   在构建好的知识图谱上运行Personalized PageRank算法，以查询节点为个性化起始点。
    *   PPR算法能有效地在图谱中传播激活，识别与查询最相关的、多跳连接的节点和路径。
    *   根据PPR得分，检索出最相关的知识片段（ passages）。
4.  **LLM合成与答案生成 (LLM Synthesis and Answer Generation)**:
    *   检索到的知识片段被输入到LLM中。
    *   LLM利用这些上下文信息生成最终的答案，实现深度知识整合。

## Comparison with Previous Work

- **vs. 传统RAG方法 (e.g., BM25, Contriever, ColBERTv2)**:
    *   传统RAG主要依赖于文本相似度匹配进行单步检索，难以有效处理多跳和需要复杂推理的问答。
    *   HippoRAG通过知识图谱和PPR实现多跳知识整合，显著提升了在复杂问答上的性能，最高提升20%。
- **vs. 迭代检索方法 (e.g., IRCoT)**:
    *   IRCoT等迭代方法通过多次检索逐步构建答案，虽然能处理多跳问题，但计算成本高昂且速度较慢。
    *   HippoRAG的单步检索在性能上与IRCoT相当或更优，但成本降低10-30倍，速度提高6-13倍。
- **vs. 仅使用LLM进行知识图谱构建**:
    *   HippoRAG不仅利用LLM构建KG，更重要的是将KG与PPR算法结合，实现动态、上下文感知的知识检索，而非静态的图谱查询。
- **vs. 仅使用PageRank或图神经网络**:
    *   HippoRAG的Personalized PageRank是基于LLM生成的查询节点进行个性化传播，而非全局或预定义的节点重要性，使其更具上下文相关性。

## Motivation

Mammalian brains have evolved to store vast amounts of knowledge and continuously integrate new information while avoiding catastrophic forgetting, enabling them to thrive in dynamic environments.

Despite the impressive accomplishments of large language models (LLMs), even with retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), they still struggle to efficiently and effectively integrate a large amount of new experiences after pre-training.

Existing methods often prove inefficient and limited in performance when tackling multi-hop question answering and scenarios requiring deep knowledge integration.

## Contribution

- **Neurobiologically Inspired Retrieval Framework**: Introduces HippoRAG, mimicking the hippocampal indexing theory of human long-term memory to enable deeper and more efficient knowledge integration for LLMs.
- **Synergistic Orchestration of LLMs, Knowledge Graphs, and Personalized PageRank**: Innovatively combines LLMs, Knowledge Graphs (KGs), and the Personalized PageRank (PPR) algorithm to simulate the distinct roles of the neocortex and hippocampus in human memory.
- **Remarkable Performance Improvement on Multi-hop QA**: HippoRAG outperforms state-of-the-art methods by up to 20% on multi-hop question answering tasks.
- **High Efficiency and Cost-Effectiveness**: Single-step retrieval with HippoRAG achieves comparable or better performance than iterative retrieval methods like IRCoT, while being 10-30 times cheaper and 6-13 times faster.
- **Ability to Tackle Novel Scenarios**: The method can address new types of complex scenarios that are beyond the reach of existing approaches.

## Method

HippoRAG mimics the human hippocampal memory mechanism through the following steps:

1.  **Knowledge Graph Construction**:
    *   An LLM (e.g., GPT-3.5 Turbo) is used to extract triples (subject-predicate-object) from raw text corpora to build an initial knowledge graph.
    *   Entity linking and coreference resolution ensure consistency of entities within the graph.
    *   Synonymy edges are added to connect semantically similar nodes, enhancing graph connectivity.
2.  **Query Expansion and Graph Activation**:
    *   For a given query, the LLM first generates relevant entities and concepts to serve as initial query nodes.
    *   These query nodes are activated within the knowledge graph, acting as starting points for Personalized PageRank.
3.  **Personalized PageRank (PPR) Retrieval**:
    *   The Personalized PageRank algorithm is run on the constructed knowledge graph, using the query nodes as personalized starting points.
    *   PPR effectively propagates activation through the graph, identifying multi-hop connected nodes and paths most relevant to the query.
    *   The most relevant passages are retrieved based on their PPR scores.
4.  **LLM Synthesis and Answer Generation**:
    *   The retrieved knowledge passages are fed into the LLM.
    *   The LLM utilizes this contextual information to generate the final answer, achieving deep knowledge integration.

## Comparison with Previous Work

- **vs. Traditional RAG methods (e.g., BM25, Contriever, ColBERTv2)**:
    *   Traditional RAG primarily relies on text similarity matching for single-step retrieval, struggling with multi-hop and complex reasoning questions.
    *   HippoRAG integrates multi-hop knowledge through KGs and PPR, significantly boosting performance on complex QA, with improvements up to 20%.
- **vs. Iterative Retrieval methods (e.g., IRCoT)**:
    *   Iterative methods like IRCoT build answers incrementally through multiple retrieval steps, which can handle multi-hop problems but are computationally expensive and slow.
    *   HippoRAG's single-step retrieval achieves comparable or superior performance to IRCoT, while being 10-30 times cheaper and 6-13 times faster.
- **vs. LLM-only Knowledge Graph Construction**:
    *   HippoRAG not only uses LLMs for KG construction but crucially integrates the KG with the PPR algorithm for dynamic, context-aware knowledge retrieval, rather than static graph queries.
- **vs. PageRank or Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) alone**:
    *   HippoRAG's Personalized PageRank is tailored by LLM-generated query nodes for personalized propagation, making it more contextually relevant than global or pre-defined node importance.

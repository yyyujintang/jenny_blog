## Motivation

现有的视觉与语言任务数据集在任务复杂性、动作空间和语言指令方面与现实世界应用存在较大差距。这些数据集通常只涉及简单的、单步的或可逆的任务，难以评估AI代理在真实场景下的泛化能力和鲁棒性。

为了推动具身AI领域的发展，需要一个能够模拟真实家庭环境、包含复杂多步任务和自然语言指令的基准。这样的基准可以更好地测试AI代理理解长指令、执行复杂操作以及处理非可逆状态变化的能力。

## Contribution

- **ALFRED基准数据集**: 提出了一个包含25k条自然语言指令和专家演示的基准，用于家庭任务的具身AI学习。
- **复杂任务设计**: 任务具有长序列、组合性强、包含非可逆状态变化等特点，更接近真实世界应用。
- **多粒度语言指令**: 包含高层目标（如“冲洗马克杯并放入咖啡机”）和低层指令（如“走到右边的咖啡机”）。
- **评估基线模型**: 展示了现有具身视觉-语言任务的基线模型在ALFRED上的表现不佳，突出了该基准的挑战性。

## Method

ALFRED主要是一个数据集和基准的构建，而非提出新的模型方法。其核心在于任务和数据的设计：

1.  **环境构建**: 使用交互式视觉环境（如AI2-THOR），模拟真实的家庭场景。
2.  **任务设计**:
    *   定义了多种日常家庭任务，例如烹饪、清洁、整理等。
    *   任务被设计为长序列、多步骤，并且包含非可逆的状态变化（例如，一旦物品被清洗，就不能“未清洗”）。
3.  **语言指令收集**:
    *   收集了25k条自然语言指令，涵盖了从高层目标到低层具体动作的多种粒度。
    *   这些指令由人类专家根据任务演示生成。
4.  **专家演示**: 记录了人类专家在虚拟环境中完成任务的动作序列，作为模型的训练数据和评估标准。
5.  **基线模型评估**: 采用了一个基于现有具身视觉-语言任务的Seq2Seq模型作为基线，评估其在ALFRED上的性能。模型输入包括视觉观察和语言指令，输出是动作序列。

## Motivation

Existing vision-and-language task datasets often fall short in terms of task complexity, action space, and language instructions when compared to real-world applications. These datasets typically involve simple, single-step, or reversible tasks, making it difficult to evaluate the generalization capabilities and robustness of AI agents in realistic scenarios.

To advance the field of embodied AI, there is a need for a benchmark that simulates realistic household environments, includes complex multi-step tasks, and features natural language instructions. Such a benchmark can better test an AI agent's ability to understand long directives, perform intricate operations, and handle non-reversible state changes.

## Contribution

- **ALFRED Benchmark Dataset**: Introduces a benchmark with 25k natural language directives and expert demonstrations for embodied AI learning in household tasks.
- **Complex Task Design**: Tasks are characterized by long sequences, high compositionality, and non-reversible state changes, bringing them closer to real-world applications.
- **Multi-granularity Language Instructions**: Includes both high-level goals (e.g., "Rinse off a mug and place it in the coffee maker") and low-level instructions (e.g., "Walk to the coffee maker on the right").
- **Baseline Model Evaluation**: Demonstrates that a baseline model based on existing embodied vision-and-language tasks performs poorly on ALFRED, highlighting the benchmark's challenging nature.

## Method

ALFRED primarily focuses on the construction of a dataset and benchmark rather than proposing a new model method. Its core lies in the design of tasks and data:

1.  **Environment Construction**: Utilizes interactive visual environments (e.g., AI2-THOR) to simulate realistic household scenarios.
2.  **Task Design**:
    *   Defines various everyday household tasks, such as cooking, cleaning, and organizing.
    *   Tasks are designed to be long-sequence, multi-step, and include non-reversible state changes (e.g., once an item is cleaned, it cannot be "uncleaned").
3.  **Language Instruction Collection**:
    *   Collected 25k natural language instructions, covering various granularities from high-level goals to low-level specific actions.
    *   These instructions were generated by human experts based on task demonstrations.
4.  **Expert Demonstrations**: Records sequences of actions performed by human experts in the virtual environment, serving as training data and evaluation standards for models.
5.  **Baseline Model Evaluation**: A Seq2Seq model, based on existing embodied vision-and-language tasks, is used as a baseline to evaluate its performance on ALFRED. The model takes visual observations and language instructions as input and outputs action sequences.

## Comparison with Previous Work

- **vs. TACoS, R2R, Touchdown**: These datasets primarily focus on language localization in videos or path navigation, with lower task complexity, typically not involving object interaction and non-reversible environmental state changes. ALFRED emphasizes embodied interaction and complex multi-step tasks.
- **vs. EQA, Matterport EQA, IQA**: These are visual question answering datasets. While they involve visual understanding, they typically do not require agents to execute a sequence of actions to alter the environment's state. ALFRED demands agents to perform embodied actions based on instructions.
- **vs. VirtualHome, VSP**: While these datasets also involve embodied interaction and complex environments, ALFRED offers a more comprehensive challenge in terms of language instruction granularity, task compositionality, and non-reversible state changes, and boasts a larger dataset size.
- **vs. Existing Embodied Vision-and-Language Tasks**: ALFRED surpasses existing datasets in terms of sequence length, action space, and language complexity, aiming to bridge the gap between research benchmarks and real-world applications.

我们提出了一个核心问题：VLM智能体能否通过显式视觉状态推理来构建内部世界模型？

现有方法在处理多轮VLM智能体的复杂视觉环境时，往往难以有效地进行状态估计和转换建模。

## Motivation

A key challenge in training Vision-Language Model (VLM) agents, compared to Language Model (LLM) agents, lies in the shift from textual states to complex visual observations. This transition introduces partial observability and demands robust world modeling.

We ask: Can VLM agents construct internal world models through explicit visual state reasoning?

Existing methods often struggle with effective state estimation and transition modeling when dealing with the complex visual environments of multi-turn VLM agents.

## Contribution

- **Architectural Enforcement and Reward**: Architecturally enforce and reward the agent's reasoning process via RL, formulating it as a POMDP.
- **Reasoning Decomposition**: Decompose the agent's reasoning into "State Estimation" ("what is the current state?") and "Transition Modeling" ("what comes next?"), which is critical for success.
- **Internal Belief Representation Investigation**: Reveal that the optimal internal belief representation is task-dependent: Natural Language excels at capturing semantic relationships in general tasks, while Structured formats are indispensable for precise manipulation and control.
- **World Modeling Reward**: Design a World Modeling Reward that provides dense, turn-level supervision for accurate state prediction.
- **Bi-Level GAE**: Introduce Bi-Level General Advantage Estimation (Bi-Level GAE) for turn-aware credit assignment.
- **VAGEN Framework**: Propose the VAGEN framework, a scalable system for training and analyzing multi-turn VLM agents in diverse visual environments.

## Method

### 1. POMDP Formulation

We formulate the VLM agent's reasoning process as a Partially Observable Markov Decision Process (POMDP) to address the partial observability introduced by visual observations.

### 2. Reasoning Decomposition

The agent's core reasoning is decomposed into two critical components:
- **State Estimation**: The agent learns to answer "what is the current state?".
- **Transition Modeling**: The agent learns to predict "what comes next?".

### 3. Internal Belief Representation

We investigate different approaches for representing internal beliefs:
- **Natural Language**: Effective for capturing semantic relationships in general tasks.
- **Structured Formats**: Indispensable for tasks requiring precise manipulation and control.

### 4. World Modeling Reward

A novel dense reward mechanism is designed:
- **Turn-level Supervision**: Provides turn-level supervision for accurate state prediction by the agent.

### 5. Bi-Level General Advantage Estimation (Bi-Level GAE)

Bi-Level GAE is introduced to optimize credit assignment:
- **Turn-aware**: Ensures that rewards are effectively attributed to specific turn actions that lead to success.

### 6. VAGEN Framework

All experiments and methods are integrated within the VAGEN framework, a scalable system for training and analyzing multi-turn VLM agents.

## Comparison with Previous Work

- **vs. LLM agents**: VAGEN specifically addresses the transition from textual states to complex visual observations in VLM agents, tackling partial observability and world modeling challenges not present in LLM agents.
- **vs. Untrained VLM agents**: VAGEN achieves a 3x performance improvement through explicit visual state reasoning and reinforcement learning, significantly outperforming untrained VLM counterparts.
- **vs. Proprietary reasoning models (GPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Claude 4.5)**: VAGEN outperforms these proprietary models across multiple benchmarks, demonstrating its superior capability in visual state reasoning.
- **vs. Implicit world modeling approaches**: VAGEN explicitly enforces and rewards world model reasoning architecturally, rather than relying on implicit learning, leading to increased transparency and control.

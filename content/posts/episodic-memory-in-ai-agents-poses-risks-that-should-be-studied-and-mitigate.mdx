## Motivation

Most current AI models possess limited ability to store and later retrieve a record or representation of their own actions.
In human cognition, episodic memories are crucial for both recalling the past and planning for the future.
Similarly, the ability to form and utilize episodic memories would enable a broad range of improved capabilities in an AI agent that interacts with and takes actions in the world.

Researchers have begun directing more attention to developing memory abilities in AI models, and it is likely that models with such capability will become widespread in the near future.
This could, in some ways, contribute to making such AI agents safer by enabling users to better monitor, understand, and control their actions.
However, as a new capability with wide applications, we argue that it will also introduce significant new risks that researchers should begin to study and address.

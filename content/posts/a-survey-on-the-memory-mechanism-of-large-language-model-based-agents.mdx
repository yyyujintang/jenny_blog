## Motivation

LLM-based agents因其自我演化能力，在解决需要长期复杂交互的现实世界问题中备受关注。
支持agent与环境交互的关键组件是其记忆机制。
尽管现有研究提出了许多有前景的记忆机制，但它们分散在不同论文中，缺乏系统性综述来总结和比较这些工作。

这种分散性阻碍了对通用有效设计模式的抽象，从而未能有效启发未来的研究。
因此，需要一个全面的综述来填补这一空白，系统地梳理LLM-based agents的记忆机制。

## Motivation

LLM-based agents have garnered significant attention due to their self-evolving capabilities, which are crucial for solving real-world problems requiring long-term and complex agent-environment interactions.
The memory mechanism is a key component enabling these agent-environment interactions.
While many promising memory mechanisms have been proposed, they are scattered across various papers, lacking a systematic review to summarize and compare them holistically.

This fragmentation hinders the abstraction of common and effective design patterns, thus failing to inspire future research effectively.
Therefore, a comprehensive survey is needed to bridge this gap by systematically organizing the memory mechanisms of LLM-based agents.

## Contribution

*   **Systematic Survey**: The first comprehensive survey on memory mechanisms for LLM-based agents, covering "what is" and "why do we need" memory.
*   **Design and Evaluation Framework**: A systematic review of how to design and evaluate memory modules, abstracting common design patterns.
*   **Application Scenario Analysis**: Presents the significant role of memory modules in various agent applications.
*   **Limitations and Future Directions**: Analyzes the limitations of existing work and outlines important future research directions.
*   **Repository**: Creation and maintenance of a GitHub repository (https://github.com/nuster1128/LLM_Agent_Memory_Survey) to keep up with the latest advancements in the field.

## Method

This survey adopts the following structure and methodology to systematically analyze the memory mechanisms of LLM-based agents:

1.  **Definition and Necessity**: Initially discusses the definition of memory in LLM-based agents and its importance.
2.  **Memory Module Design**:
    *   **Memory Sources**: Explores the sources of memory information an agent can leverage, including inside-trial, cross-trial, and external knowledge.
    *   **Memory Forms**: Analyzes the representation forms of memory, such as textual forms (complete, recent, retrieved, external) and parametric forms (fine-tuning, editing).
    *   **Memory Operations**: Examines mechanisms for memory writing, management (merging, reflection, forgetting), and reading.
3.  **Memory Module Evaluation**: Discusses methods and metrics for evaluating the effectiveness of memory modules.
4.  **Application Scenarios**: Showcases specific examples and the critical role of memory modules in various LLM-based agent applications.
5.  **Challenges and Future Directions**: Summarizes the limitations of existing work and provides insights into future research opportunities and directions.

## Comparison with Previous Work

*   **vs. Existing Dispersed Studies**: Most existing works focus on proposing single or specific types of memory mechanisms, lacking a systematic overview of the entire field. This survey integrates these dispersed studies to provide a unified perspective.
*   **vs. Lack of Design Pattern Abstraction**: Previous research has failed to abstract general and effective memory design patterns at a macroscopic level. This survey aims to fill this gap, guiding future designs.
*   **vs. Lack of Evaluation Method Summarization**: There has been no systematic summary of evaluation methods for memory modules in prior literature. This survey incorporates evaluation methods into its framework, helping researchers better measure the effectiveness of memory mechanisms.
*   **vs. Lack of Future Direction Guidance**: Previous studies typically focus only on improving their own methods. In contrast, this survey identifies important future research directions in the field by analyzing existing limitations.

## Motivation

视觉语言模型（VLMs）在多模态理解方面表现出色，但其文本解码方式迫使它们将视觉推理“口头化”，这限制了它们在需要视觉想象力任务上的表现。

最近的一些尝试通过训练VLM生成显式图像来解决此问题，但这种方法通常需要大量的图像生成预训练，反而会损害模型的推理能力。

受人类通过心理意象（即内部构建和操作视觉线索）进行推理的启发，我们探索VLM是否可以在不生成显式图像的情况下，通过交错的多模态轨迹进行推理。

## Contribution

- **Machine Mental Imagery框架 (Mirage)**：提出了一种新颖的框架，通过在VLM解码过程中引入隐式视觉token来增强多模态推理，无需生成像素级图像。
- **潜藏视觉token机制**：当模型选择“视觉思考”时，它会将隐藏状态重新铸造为下一个token，从而在不生成显式图像的情况下继续多模态轨迹。
- **两阶段训练策略**：
    1. **蒸馏监督**：首先通过从真实图像嵌入中蒸馏来监督潜藏token。
    2. **文本监督与强化学习**：随后切换到纯文本监督，使潜藏轨迹与任务目标紧密对齐，并通过强化学习进一步增强多模态推理能力。
- **无需显式图像生成**：证明了在不生成显式图像的情况下，也能实现更强大的多模态推理能力。

## Method

Mirage框架通过以下步骤实现其多模态推理能力：

1.  **潜藏视觉token的引入**：
    *   在VLM的解码过程中，除了普通的文本token，Mirage还引入了“潜藏视觉token”。
    *   当模型需要进行视觉推理时，它会选择生成这些潜藏视觉token，而不是文本token。
2.  **隐藏状态重塑**：
    *   当模型决定“视觉思考”时，它会将其当前的隐藏状态重新映射或“重铸”为下一个潜藏视觉token。
    *   这个过程允许模型在内部操作视觉信息，而无需将其转换为像素级的图像。
3.  **多模态轨迹的延续**：
    *   通过交替生成文本token和潜藏视觉token，模型能够构建一个“交错的多模态轨迹”。
    *   这条轨迹包含了文本推理和视觉思考的序列，使得模型能够进行更复杂的、需要视觉想象力的推理。

### 训练阶段

1.  **蒸馏监督 (Distillation Supervision)**：
    *   **目标**：使潜藏视觉token能够有效地编码视觉信息。
    *   **方法**：利用预训练的图像编码器生成真实图像的嵌入（ground-truth image embeddings）。
    *   **过程**：将模型生成的潜藏视觉token与这些真实图像嵌入进行对齐，通过蒸馏损失进行监督，确保潜藏token能够捕捉到图像的关键特征。
2.  **文本监督 (Text-Only Supervision)**：
    *   **目标**：使潜藏轨迹与具体的任务目标紧密对齐。
    *   **方法**：在蒸馏阶段之后，训练转向纯文本监督。
    *   **过程**：模型在生成潜藏视觉token时，其目标是优化最终的文本输出，确保视觉思考过程有助于生成正确的文本答案。
3.  **强化学习 (Reinforcement Learning - RL)**：
    *   **目标**：进一步增强多模态推理能力，尤其是在复杂任务上。
    *   **方法**：引入RL阶段，通过奖励机制鼓励模型生成更有助于解决任务的潜藏视觉轨迹。
    *   **过程**：RL可以帮助模型探索更有效的视觉思考策略，从而在没有显式视觉反馈的情况下提升性能。

## Comparison with Previous Work

-   **vs. 显式图像生成方法**：
    *   **Mirage**：无需生成像素级图像，避免了图像生成预训练的复杂性和计算开销，也避免了其可能对推理能力造成的负面影响。
    *   **显式图像生成**：需要大量的图像生成预训练，且可能限制模型的推理能力。
-   **vs. 纯文本解码VLM**：
    *   **Mirage**：通过引入潜藏视觉token，允许模型进行内部的“视觉思考”，从而更好地处理需要视觉想象力的任务。
    *   **纯文本解码VLM**：被迫将所有视觉推理“口头化”，在需要复杂视觉想象的任务上表现受限。
-   **vs. MVoT (Multimodal-CoT)**:
    *   **Mirage**: 专注于通过内部潜藏视觉token进行推理，不依赖于外部工具或显式多模态思维链。
    *   **MVoT**: 依赖于生成显式的多模态思维链，可能包括文本和图像，但图像生成仍是其瓶颈。

## Motivation

Vision-language models (VLMs) excel at multimodal understanding, yet their text-only decoding forces them to verbalize visual reasoning. This limitation restricts their performance on tasks that demand visual imagination.

Recent attempts train VLMs to render explicit images. However, this often requires heavy image-generation pre-training, which can paradoxically hinder the models' core reasoning abilities.

Inspired by how humans reason with mental imagery—the internal construction and manipulation of visual cues—we investigate whether VLMs can reason through interleaved multimodal trajectories without producing explicit images.

## Contribution

-   **Machine Mental Imagery Framework (Mirage)**: Introduces a novel framework that augments VLM decoding with latent visual tokens to enhance multimodal reasoning without generating pixel-level images.
-   **Latent Visual Token Mechanism**: When the model chooses to "think visually," it recasts its hidden states as next tokens, thereby continuing a multimodal trajectory without generating explicit images.
-   **Two-Stage Training Strategy**:
    1.  **Distillation Supervision**: Initially supervises latent tokens by distilling from ground-truth image embeddings.
    2.  **Text-Only Supervision and Reinforcement Learning**: Subsequently switches to text-only supervision to align the latent trajectory tightly with the task objective, further enhancing multimodal reasoning through reinforcement learning.
-   **Elimination of Explicit Image Generation**: Demonstrates that stronger multimodal reasoning can be achieved without the need for explicit image generation.

## Method

The Mirage framework achieves its multimodal reasoning capabilities through the following steps:

1.  **Introduction of Latent Visual Tokens**:
    *   During the VLM's decoding process, Mirage introduces "latent visual tokens" alongside ordinary text tokens.
    *   When the model needs to perform visual reasoning, it chooses to generate these latent visual tokens instead of text tokens.
2.  **Hidden State Recasting**:
    *   When the model decides to "think visually," it remaps or "recasts" its current hidden states as the next latent visual token.
    *   This process allows the model to internally manipulate visual information without converting it into pixel-level images.
3.  **Continuation of Multimodal Trajectory**:
    *   By alternately generating text tokens and latent visual tokens, the model constructs an "interleaved multimodal trajectory."
    *   This trajectory encompasses a sequence of textual reasoning and visual thinking, enabling the model to perform more complex reasoning that requires visual imagination.

### Training Stages

1.  **Distillation Supervision**:
    *   **Objective**: To enable latent visual tokens to effectively encode visual information.
    *   **Method**: Utilizes pre-trained image encoders to generate ground-truth image embeddings.
    *   **Process**: The latent visual tokens generated by the model are aligned with these ground-truth image embeddings through a distillation loss, ensuring that the latent tokens capture key visual features.
2.  **Text-Only Supervision**:
    *   **Objective**: To tightly align the latent trajectory with specific task objectives.
    *   **Method**: After the distillation stage, training shifts to purely text-based supervision.
    *   **Process**: When generating latent visual tokens, the model's objective is to optimize the final text output, ensuring that the visual thinking process contributes to generating correct textual answers.
3.  **Reinforcement Learning (RL)**:
    *   **Objective**: To further enhance multimodal reasoning capabilities, especially for complex tasks.
    *   **Method**: An RL stage is introduced, using a reward mechanism to encourage the model to generate latent visual trajectories that are more conducive to solving the task.
    *   **Process**: RL helps the model explore more effective visual thinking strategies, thereby improving performance without explicit visual feedback.

## Comparison with Previous Work

-   **vs. Explicit Image Generation Methods**:
    *   **Mirage**: Eliminates the need for pixel-level image generation, avoiding the complexity and computational overhead of image-generation pre-training, and its potential negative impact on reasoning ability.
    *   **Explicit Image Generation**: Requires extensive image generation pre-training and can limit the model's reasoning capabilities.
-   **vs. Text-Only Decoding VLMs**:
    *   **Mirage**: Introduces latent visual tokens, allowing the model to perform internal "visual thinking," which better handles tasks requiring visual imagination.
    *   **Text-Only Decoding VLMs**: Forced to verbalize all visual reasoning, leading to limited performance on tasks demanding complex visual imagination.
-   **vs. MVoT (Multimodal-CoT)**:
    *   **Mirage**: Focuses on internal reasoning via latent visual tokens, not relying on external tools or explicit multimodal chains of thought.
    *   **MVoT**: Relies on generating explicit multimodal chains of thought, which may include text and images, but image generation remains a bottleneck.

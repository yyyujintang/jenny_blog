## Motivation

LLM-based agents在数字环境中作为自主控制器展现出强大潜力，但处理长序列动作和动态内容时面临挑战，例如web导航。

现有LLM agents在长序列规划方面存在两大问题：
* 在线执行时，它们常因新信息而偏离目标，缺乏清晰自适应的路径。
* 在RL fine-tuning中，稀疏延迟的奖励信号使得agent难以识别成功动作，难以在长时间任务中保持连贯推理。

## Contribution

*   **子目标分解的在线规划框架**: 引入一个利用专有模型进行在线规划的agent框架，通过将复杂任务分解为可管理的子目标来解决长序列规划问题。
*   **MiRA (Milestoning your Reinforcement Learning Enhanced Agent) RL训练框架**: 提出一个使用密集、基于里程碑的奖励信号的RL训练框架，以解决RL fine-tuning中奖励稀疏和延迟的问题。

## Method

### 1. 在线规划机制
*   **子目标分解**: 将最终目标分解为一系列更小的、可管理的子目标。
*   **实时路径调整**: 当新信息出现时，agent能够动态调整其规划路径，确保始终朝着当前子目标前进。

### 2. MiRA RL训练框架
*   **里程碑奖励**: 为agent在任务执行过程中达到特定里程碑提供密集的、即时的奖励信号，而非仅仅在最终成功时才给予奖励。
*   **强化学习增强**: 利用这些密集的奖励信号来更有效地fine-tune LLM agent，使其能更好地理解哪些动作导致成功。

## Comparison with Previous Work

*   **vs. 现有LLM agents**: 我们的方法通过明确的推理时规划和里程碑奖励，解决了现有LLM agents在长序列任务中“迷失方向”和奖励稀疏的问题。
*   **vs. GPT-4-Turbo (17.6%) 和 GPT-4o (13.9%)**: MiRA框架应用于Gemma3-12B模型后，其性能（43.0%）显著超越了这些专有系统。
*   **vs. WebRL (38.4%)**: MiRA框架也超越了之前开源模型的SOTA，表明在长序列任务中具有更强的泛化能力和鲁棒性。

## Motivation

LLM-based agents have emerged as powerful autonomous controllers for digital environments, but they face challenges when handling long sequences of actions and dynamic content, such as web navigation.

Existing LLM-based agents struggle with long-horizon planning in two main ways:
*   During online execution, they often lose track as new information arrives, lacking a clear and adaptive path toward the final goal.
*   During reinforcement learning (RL) fine-tuning, sparse and delayed rewards make it difficult for agents to identify which actions lead to success, preventing them from maintaining coherent reasoning over extended tasks.

## Contribution

*   **Subgoal Decomposition for Online Planning**: We introduce an agent framework that leverages proprietary models for online planning through subgoal decomposition, addressing long-horizon planning challenges by breaking down complex tasks.
*   **MiRA (Milestoning your Reinforcement Learning Enhanced Agent) RL Training Framework**: We present an RL training framework that uses dense, milestone-based reward signals to overcome the issues of sparse and delayed rewards in RL fine-tuning.

## Method

### 1. Online Planning Mechanism
*   **Subgoal Decomposition**: The final goal is broken down into a series of smaller, more manageable subgoals.
*   **Real-time Path Adjustment**: The agent can dynamically adjust its planned path as new information emerges, ensuring it continuously progresses towards the current subgoal.

### 2. MiRA RL Training Framework
*   **Milestone-based Rewards**: Dense, immediate reward signals are provided to the agent upon reaching specific milestones during task execution, rather than only at final success.
*   **Reinforcement Learning Enhancement**: These dense reward signals are used to more effectively fine-tune the LLM agent, helping it better understand which actions lead to success.

## Comparison with Previous Work

*   **vs. Existing LLM Agents**: Our method addresses the "losing track" and sparse reward problems in long-horizon tasks for existing LLM agents through explicit inference-time planning and milestone-based rewards.
*   **vs. GPT-4-Turbo (17.6%) and GPT-4o (13.9%)**: The MiRA framework, when applied to the Gemma3-12B model, significantly outperforms these proprietary systems with a success rate of 43.0%.
*   **vs. WebRL (38.4%)**: The MiRA framework also surpasses the previous open-model state-of-the-art, demonstrating stronger generalization and robustness in long-horizon tasks.

*   **Validation of Long-Horizon Capabilities**: Our findings demonstrate that combining explicit inference-time planning with milestone-based rewards significantly improves an agent's long-horizon capabilities.

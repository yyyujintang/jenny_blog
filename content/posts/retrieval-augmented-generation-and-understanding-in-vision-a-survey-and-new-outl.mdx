## Motivation

Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) has emerged as a pivotal technique in AI, particularly for enhancing LLMs by enabling access to external knowledge sources.
In the context of AI-Generated Content (AIGC), RAG has proven invaluable by augmenting model outputs with supplementary, relevant information, thereby improving their quality.
Recently, the potential of RAG has extended beyond natural language processing, with emerging methods integrating retrieval-augmented strategies into the computer vision (CV) domain to address limitations of relying solely on internal model knowledge.

## Contribution

- **Comprehensive Review**: Provides a comprehensive review of the current state of retrieval-augmented techniques in CV, focusing on visual understanding and visual generation.
- **Task Categorization**: Systematically reviews visual understanding tasks ranging from basic image recognition to complex applications like medical report generation and multimodal question answering; also examines RAG's application in image, video, and 3D generation.
- **Embodied AI Applications**: Explores recent advancements of RAG in embodied AI, with a particular focus on planning, task execution, multimodal perception, interaction, and specialized domains.
- **Limitations and Future Directions**: Highlights key limitations of current approaches and proposes future research directions to drive the development of this promising area.

## Method

As a survey paper, this work primarily organizes and presents its content through the following structure:
